Simone Ramello

Ciao! My name is Simone (he/him), and I am a mathematician and occasional storyteller. I am currently based in Münster, Germany. ciao@simoneramello.it - name.surname@uni-muenster.de

Research

By day, I am a third-year PhD student at the Institute for Mathematical Logic of the University of Münster, under the supervision of Franziska Jahnke and Martin Hils. My research sits between logic and algebra, using techniques from model theory to tackle questions about valued fields. In particular, I like to think about valued fields with extra structure (especially endomorphisms), and definability of henselian valuations.

Science Communication

I am also active in science communication and outreach. Making mathematical research accessible and relatable is not just a matter of improving the societal view of the subject. It is also, in the age of data, a fundamental problem for the well-being of democracy. We must be critical and mindful of narratives around mathematics, and who gets to do it.
I am a member of Meet Science.
